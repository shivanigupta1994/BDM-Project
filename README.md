# GrocerAnalytics: Kamal Grocery Store Deep Dive 🛒📊

## 🎓 Project Overview
This is my Capstone Project for the **IIT-Madras Business Data Management** program. It is a comprehensive case study and data analysis of "Kamal Grocery Store," a medium-sized retail business established in 2014. 

The objective was to extract real-world, unorganized primary data from physical bill books, clean it, and perform a deep-dive business analysis to solve the store's challenges with profitability, sales, and inventory management.

## 🛠️ Tools & Methodology
* **Data Collection:** Extracted and digitized primary data for 15 key SKUs from unorganized physical records.
* **Data Cleaning & Analysis:** Performed entirely in **Microsoft Excel**.
* **Financial Modeling:** Developed a Normalized Fixed Cost Analysis and a comprehensive Balance Sheet to evaluate overall business health.

## 🔍 Key Business Insights

### 1. Sales & Revenue
* The store averages a daily revenue of ₹25,846.
* **Pareto Principle:** Just 9 items (including Chakki Atta, Cooking Oil, Ghee, and Makhana) contribute to approximately 80% of the total revenue.

### 2. Profitability Gaps
* **Critical Finding:** The business operates on a very low Net Profit Ratio of 1.91% and a Gross Profit Ratio of 7.94%.
* **Recommendation:** High-revenue items like Ghee (5% margin), Makhana (10%), and Cashew (6%) require immediate pricing optimization to maximize net profit.

### 3. Inventory Mismanagement
* Data analysis confirmed the owner's suspicion of severe overstocking. 
* Items like Raw Rice, Sela Rice, Moong Dal, Cooking Oil, and Elaichi have stock levels significantly exceeding their average sales pace, which ties up essential working capital.
